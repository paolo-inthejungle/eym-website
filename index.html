Why join us

Why Join Us

The only way to build a European future is to take personal responsibility for change — and the new generations have the strength to do it.

Shape Your Identity Through Community

Only by knowing one another, acknowledging different realities and sharing a common political journey can we truly feel like citizens of Europe and create the conditions to act together.

Debate to Propose

Social, cultural and political diversity is not a limitation — it is the fertile ground on which to cultivate dialogue and give rise to shared political positions, capable of combining unity of vision with respect for differences.

Don't think as a voter,
think as a doer!

United by a pragmatic, positive approach to finding solutions together. Make your contribution and be an active part of change — join the European Youth Movement.

Our strategic plan

Objectives

We pursue concrete goals to make young Europeans' voices heard at every level of governance.

1
Expand the network across Europe

Recruit members and national coordinators in every EU member state to build a truly pan-European movement.

2
Publish policy proposals

Produce rigorous position papers and policy briefs on the key EU challenges, submitted to European institutions.

3
Foster political education

Organise training events, simulations and debates to empower young citizens with political knowledge and skills.

4
Engage with EU institutions

Establish formal dialogue channels with the European Parliament, Commission and other EU bodies.

5
Build a media presence

Grow our communications through social media, a newsletter, articles and media appearances to broaden our reach.

Stay informed

Subscribe to our newsletter — open to everyone, members and non-members alike.

🛡️ Foreign Policy & Defense

The Foreign Policy & Defense working group develops EYM's positions on European strategic autonomy, transatlantic relations, EU enlargement and conflict prevention.

Our proposed solutions include:

  • Strengthening the EU's Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP) through majority voting
  • Increasing investment in joint European defence capabilities and reducing fragmentation
  • Deepening EU–NATO cooperation while maintaining European strategic independence
  • Advancing EU enlargement to the Western Balkans and Eastern neighbourhood with credible reform conditionality
  • Creating a dedicated youth consultation mechanism within EU foreign policy institutions

Position papers and detailed proposals from this group are published in the Policies section.

⚡ Energy & Environment

The Energy & Environment working group works on accelerating the green transition, ensuring energy security and advocating for climate justice across Europe.

Our proposed solutions include:

  • Accelerating the phase-out of fossil fuels with a fair, just transition plan for workers and regions
  • Strengthening the EU Emissions Trading System and extending carbon pricing across all sectors
  • Investing in pan-European renewable energy infrastructure and grid interconnections
  • Introducing a European Climate Youth Budget to fund green innovation led by young people
  • Embedding biodiversity protection in all EU sectoral policies

Position papers and detailed proposals from this group are published in the Policies section.

⚖️ Justice

The Justice working group defends the rule of law, fundamental rights and democratic institutions across Europe, and proposes systemic reforms to ensure accountability and fairness.

Our proposed solutions include:

  • Strengthening the EU Rule of Law mechanism with binding financial conditionality
  • Creating an independent EU anti-corruption body with investigative powers
  • Reforming the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights to include digital rights
  • Improving access to justice for young people through legal aid and simplified procedures
  • Promoting judicial independence benchmarks as part of EU accession criteria

Position papers and detailed proposals from this group are published in the Policies section.

🌍 Immigration & Human Rights

The Immigration & Human Rights working group advocates for humane, rights-based migration policies and the protection of fundamental freedoms for all people in Europe.

Our proposed solutions include:

  • Reforming the Common European Asylum System to guarantee fair, swift and dignified procedures
  • Establishing a permanent EU resettlement mechanism with mandatory member state participation
  • Combating racism, xenophobia and discrimination through binding EU anti-discrimination law
  • Promoting integration policies co-designed with migrant communities
  • Strengthening protection for unaccompanied minors across the EU

Position papers and detailed proposals from this group are published in the Policies section.
